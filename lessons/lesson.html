language learning

We provide resources to help you learn Kikuyu, Kalenjin, Luo and Somali languages. Our website has lessons, videos, audio files, quizzes and more to support your language learning journey.

conversation practice

After studying the grammar and vocabulary, it's important to practice having real conversations. Our site lets you chat with our chabot to improve your speaking and listening abilities.

Varied culture study courses

Heritage provides immersive language and cultural study courses ranging from beginner to advanced in countries around the world.

Kikuyu

Kikuyu originated among the Kikuyu people in central Kenya. Over 6 million native speakers make it the most widely spoken language in Kenya.

View courses

Kalenjin

Kalenjin is spoken by the Kalenjin people who live on the western side of the Rift Valley in Kenya. Around 5 million people speak Kalenjin as their first language.

View courses

Luo

Luo is spoken by the Luo people located mainly in western Kenya along Lake Victoria. Over 4 million native Luo speakers make it one of Kenya's most common languages.
